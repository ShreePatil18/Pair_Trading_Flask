Pair
Trading

A "pair" is a combination of two assets that are traded together in pair trading based on their historical price relationship. Even though their values may occasionally diverge, these two assets are often chosen because they have a history of moving together in a fairly predictable way.

When and
Where

Pair trading is commonly used when two assets have a historical correlation but temporarily deviate from their typical relationship. Traders look for opportunities to enter the market when the spread between the two assets is expected to converge.

How and
Who

Pair trading involves taking a long position in one asset and a short position in another asset that are historically correlated. The goal is to profit from the convergence of their prices when they deviate from their typical relationship.
